🎭 Dignity in Drag

A Soliloquy on Posture, Performance, and Becoming

“You’ll never find rainbows if you’re looking down.” — Charlie Chaplin
Dressing up for a job interview after eviction — that’s not deception. That’s dignity in drag. That’s posture as prayer.

We talk about "posturing" like it’s a lie. Like it’s fake. Like it’s vanity.

But what if posturing is actually proto-truth — a gesture toward the self you aren’t yet allowed to be?

The Tramp walks with flair not to impress — but to survive. His wobble is not weakness. It’s ritual. His cane and bowler aren’t props — they’re **armor**.

In a collapsing world, you keep walking funny — not because it’s efficient, but because it’s the last thing you have that’s yours.

“I remain soft in the face of a hard world.” — anonymous intern in a suit two sizes too big

Maybe we posture because we still **hope to be recognized**. Maybe we perform because we believe there’s still an **audience for becoming**. Maybe putting on the tie, the lashes, the deodorant... isn’t vanity at all — it’s **resistance theater**.

Posturing ≠ Pretending

It’s prefiguring.
It’s showing up *as if* the world were just.
As if someone might say:

“You look ready.”
“We’ve been waiting for you.”
“Come in.”

And maybe that’s enough.
